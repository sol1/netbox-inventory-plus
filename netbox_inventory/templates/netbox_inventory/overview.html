Welcome to NetBox Inventory Plus

This is a plugin that allows you to represent and manage your inventory within NetBox. Keep track of your assets, their locations, and their status. Here is how you can get started:

  1. Start by creating an Asset. Assets are representations of devices, modules, racks or other inventory items in your network. Assets are required to define a device/module/inventory item/rack type and a status. If you are unsure on the Asset's status, 'Planned' is a good default.
  2. Create a Supplier. Suppliers are the vendors of the physical products your assets are based on. For example, if you have a Cisco switch from Cisco Australia, the supplier name would be 'Cisco Australia' and the slug would be 'cisco-australia'.
  3. Create a BOM. A BOM (Bill of Materials) is a list of assets with the intention of being purchased. BOMs are required to define a name and a status. If you are unsure on the BOM's status, 'Planned' is a good default.
    Note: BOMs are not required to be created in order to create purchases. BOMs are a way to group assets together for the purpose of purchasing. If you are not sure if you need a BOM, you probably don't.
  4. In the BOM list, select the BOM you just created. This will take you to the BOM details page. You will see two tables; one for the Purchases this BOM is associated with, and one for the Assets that are associated with this BOM. At the moment, both tables should be empty. Click on the Assign Existing Assets button and select the Asset you just created to add it to this BOM.
  5. Back on the BOM details page, click on the Create New Purchase button to create a new Purchase and automatically associate it with this BOM. Purchases are required to define a Supplier, a name, and a status. If you are unsure on the Purchase's status, 'Open' is a good default. Once completed, click on the Purchase to view its details page.
  6. You should see three tables; one for BOMs, one for Deliveries, and one for Assets. Where BOMs can be thought of as a list of Assets intended for purchasing, it is useful to think of a Purchase as a checklist for your BOMs. When an Asset is purchased, click the Assign Existing Assets button to add it to this Purchase. The list of Assets is filtered to only include those that are part of the associated BOM(s). Once assigned, you'll notice that the Asset's status is updated to 'Ordered' automatically.
  7. Click the Create New Delivery button to create a Delivery and assign the current Purchase to it. You should see that the Purchase has automatically been added to the Purchases field in the form. Deliveries are also required to define a name and Delivery site and location.
  8. On the Delivery details page, you'll see two tables; one for Purchases and one for Assets. When an Asset is delivered, click the Assign Existing Assets button to add it to this Delivery. Like in step 6, the list of Assets here is filtered to only include the Assets that are part of the Delivery's assigned Purchase(s). Any Assets assigned from here will automatically have their status updated to 'Stored' and their storage_location updated to the site and location of the Delivery.
